Assignment 1: Shader Live Coding

For this assignment I wanted to try and create a sort of relaxing visual accompaniament to one of my favorite piano pieces, "omake pfadlib" by Hiroyuki Sawano. Whenever I listen to this piece I visualize cooler colors and softer movement at the beginning, with more intense and frenzied movement coming in as the song becomes more emotional. I also experimented with transitioning into more warmer, passionate tones as the music progresses. The entire song is about 3 minutes long, so unfortunately this isn't a fully choreographed live-coding session, but I hope I was able to achieve a little bit of the mesmerizeing/relaxing atmosphere I was going for. Technically, I wanted to focus on creating fluid, more natural-movement, with some randomization in the color and movement either being based on time or on the audio.
